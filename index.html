Azalea

Tri-Lights - Rhododendron

Small tree or shrub featuring one bloom per stem during the spring blooming season. Azaleas should be planted just prior to blooming. The Tri-Lights variety features white blossoms with a pink hue and yellow center over medium-green foliage. They thrive in areas landscaped to provide daily variation between full sunlight and shade.

Height: 3-4'
Spread: 5-6'
Shape: Upright, rounded
Growth rate: Slow
Blooms: May/June
Zone: 4 to 7
Care level: 4 (somewhat difficult)
Sun Light: Full-Partial

Meaning
Azaleas symbolize femininity, softness, abundant beauty and intelligence, developing passion, elegance, and wealth.

Care Tips

Planting Tips

Azaleas do best with abundant water with the foliage indicating insufficient quantities; azaleas not receiving enough water will have brown-looking foliage. Hard water can turn the leaves yellow so try to utilize rainwater as much as possible.

Intersperse azaleas with perennials such as ferns, hostas, wild ginger, or shrubs native to your area.
